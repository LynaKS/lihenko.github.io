Reasons for poor connectivity

There is a long list of issues that may contribute to a poor internet connection. And if you're not somewhat tech-savvy, the chances are that you might struggle to identify the problem with your Wi-Fi connection. Yes, buffering internet can be a direct result of software malfunction or computer viruses, but there are other simpler reasons for a poor internet connection. It simply may be the case of your router box unable to cover an entire house, leaving you with several Wi-Fi dead zones across the house. Dead zones are produced when something interferes with Wi-Fi radio waves or, in some cases, when Wi-Fi cannot extend its signal through the whole building. It could be because the floorplan and building materials in old houses sometimes interfere with the Wi-Fi signal. For example, some walls in old homes contain chicken wire for support, and this could block your Wi-Fi signal. Other things that could interfere with your Wi-Fi include old cordless phones, microwave ovens, baby monitors, wireless security systems, and wireless sound systems – all of these devices are known to cause Wi-Fi issues. The list of reasons doesn't end here, but you get the main idea: it is not always your computer that is at fault for buffering connection, it's a bunch of external factors as well. But how can one improve the situation, you may ask? It is quite evident that one will not rebuild the house so as to get rid of metal wirings.
